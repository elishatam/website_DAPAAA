BECOMING A MEMBER
All persons identifying as Asian or Asian American who have earned a degree from the College or who were matriculants of a graduating class of the College are automatically considered members of the Association.

Each year, we encourage members to contribute annual dues. Membership dues go towards providing support and funding for ongoing events and initiatives as well as for special membership benefits and offerings.
  • Ongoing Events and Initiative:
    • Alumni support for undergrad activities and events, such as the Asian American Studies initiative
    • Admissions activities to increase diversity on campus
    • Fostering open dialogues with the College administration, faculty, and student leadership
    • Establishing a professional networking resource for the DAPAAA community
    • Building relationships with regional Alumni Organizations

  • Membership Benefits, Events, and Offerings:
    • Creation and distribution of quarterly DVDs or softcopy video recordings of campus events (i.e. Culture Nights, Conferences, etc.)
    • Periodical podcasts by undergrads and alumni to keep connected to the Dartmouth community
    • Organizing and hosting DAPAAA regional events (discounts available for paid members)
    • Distributions of undergraduate publications (i.e. DAPAAA Directory, Main St.)
    • Priority invitations/discounts to events
    • Paid members are eligible to vote for DAPAAA officers

  • Annual Dues As Follows:
    • Special Young Alumni Dues (Graduated within past five years) $20
    • Regular Annual Dues $30
    • Email us for more information
    • Note: 20% of membership dues will be donated to the Office of the Asian American Student Advisor via the Dartmouth College Fund. This will not only provide much needed support for the Asian Advisor's office, but also will help raise participation rates among Asian alumni.

MEMBERSHIP BENEFITS
Over the past two years, dues have been used to sponsor events that strengthen the relationship among alumni as well as the relationship between alumni and the Asian and Asian American student body on campus. The following is a list of events we've organized in our voyage towards establishing this hub for alumni communication and connection:
  • Quarterly newsletters
  • Social events in New York City, Chicago, Washington, DC, Boston, San Francisco, and Los Angeles
  • Events in NYC to welcome matriculating students
  • DAPAAA Homecoming event with undergraduates at Dartmouth
  • Real Deal Luncheon Series with the Black Alumni of Dartmouth Association (BADA) and undergraduates at Dartmouth
  • Event in Boston with Dean of Student Life Holly Sateia
  • Avon event to help raise money for breast cancer
  • Community Service work with the Dartmouth Alumni Association of NYC
  • Discussion event on "Asia and the Internet" with Professor David Kang in NYC
In 2011, we are pursuing the following projects:
  • Updating the DAPAAA Web site to be a useful source of information for alumni
  • Strengthening DAPAAA communication via newsletters and more social and academic events
  • Networking with other college Asian Pacific American alumni associations
  • Motivating support to improve Asian and Asian American resources on campus
